Data Correlations

In this notebook we will look for correlations in NBA player statistics from the 2024-2025 season. Correlations help us understand how different statistics relate to each otherâ€”for example, do players who play more minutes also score more points?

Key Concepts:

  • Correlation: A statistical measure that describes the size and direction of a relationship between two variables.
  • Correlation Matrix: A table showing correlation coefficients between variables. Each cell in the table shows the correlation between two variables.
  • Positive Correlation: As one variable increases, the other also tends to increase (e.g., Minutes Played and Points).
  • Negative Correlation: As one variable increases, the other tends to decrease.

Hover your mouse over different squares to see the variable names and how correlated they are.

The values in the matrix range from -1.00 to 1.00. A value of 1.00 means a perfect positive correlation, while -1.00 would be a perfect negative correlation. A value near 0.00 means there is little to no linear relationship between the variables.
