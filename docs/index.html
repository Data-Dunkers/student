Data Dunkers

Welcome to the Data Dunkers course for students. In this course, you'll learn how to analyze basketball data to find interesting insights about players and teams.

How to use this site

This site contains a growing set of Data Dunkers activities. Some pages are complete and ready to use, while others are planned. To help you choose where to start, activities may include short labels:

  • Intro – A good starting point. Low setup, focused on understanding or interpretation.
  • Core – Part of the main learning path. Builds skills used in later activities.
  • Stretch – Optional or exploratory. Best for extension or curiosity.

You can follow the suggested progression or jump to any activity based on your goals.

Working Spaces & Tools

What Jupyter Notebooks Are

Jupyter Notebooks are interactive workspaces where text, code, and visuals live together in one place. Students can read explanations, run code cells, see immediate results, and write their own notes alongside the work. Notebooks make it easy to explore data step by step, test ideas, and build visualizations without needing a separate programming environment. See an example notebook.

How Students Will Work Inside Notebooks

Each activity opens in a notebook that is already set up with instructions, starter code, and space to experiment. Students move through the notebook from top to bottom, running cells as they go. They can adjust variables, change chart types, or explore additional data. Mistakes are normal—students can re-run cells or reset sections as needed. The goal is to learn by trying things out and seeing what happens.

Saving Work, Adding Notes, and Sharing With Teachers

Notebooks include writable text cells where students can add reflections, answers, observations, and personal notes. Most notebook platforms (Callysto, Colab, JupyterLab) let students save a copy to their drive or download the notebook file. These saved versions become a record of their thinking and progress. Students can also insert new text cells anywhere to capture their ideas as they work.

For advanced or adventurous learners, it’s also possible to run Jupyter Notebooks locally on a Windows, macOS, or Linux computer by installing VS Code and the appropriate Jupyter extensions, giving them full control of their working environment.

Prompt to Check With Their Instructor About Submission Method

Different classrooms may use different methods for handing in work. Before starting major activities, students should ask their instructor how they should submit their notebooks—for example, by sharing a link, downloading and uploading the file, or exporting to PDF. It’s important to note that notebooks cannot be shared directly from Callysto, while Colab allows direct link-sharing, making it the preferred option whenever possible. Students who work on notebooks locally using VS Code and the Jupyter extensions can also submit files directly from their own computer. Clarifying the expected submission method early helps keep everything organized as students move through the challenges.
